# Research

## Problem Statement

Muslim parents want to raise children upon Qur'an and Sunnah, but the practical path often feels fragmented: advice lives in one place, products in another, routines are improvised, and trust/authenticity is hard to assess quickly. The result is not lack of concern. It is lack of a clear, parent-first pathway that helps families know what to learn, what to practice, what tools to choose, and what to do next.

brilliantroots should become the guided tarbiyah layer between sincere intention and household practice: a calm, trustworthy ecosystem that equips parents first, then supports children through purposeful resources.

## Research Signals

### Market Patterns

- Islamic children's learning platforms commonly lead with structure, safety, age fit, and engagement. Tiny Ummah positions around a structured library, emotional pathways, parent notes, age bands, and family reflection prompts: https://tinyummah.com/
- eTaalib emphasizes child-safe Islamic learning, parent/learner dashboards, custom curriculum, qualified tutors, and the parent's responsibility to impart beneficial knowledge: https://e-taalib.com/
- Tawheed Learning differentiates on authenticity: stories based on Qur'an and Sunnah, upon the understanding of the Companions, with no music/animations and a calm audio/story-led format: https://tawheedlearning.com/
- Adab Adventures organizes Islamic stories by age, topic, worksheets, Qur'an stories, Prophet stories, daily life, and character outcomes: https://www.adabadventures.com/
- TarbiyahOS frames the parent pain around stress, guesswork, screen time, salah habits, and structured behavior systems: https://www.tarbiyahos.com/
- My Salah Mat makes the value proposition concrete: screen-free interactive products, family trust, reviews, and product categories for Islamic learning: https://www.mysalahmat.com/
- Islamic Parenting Solutions frames digital parenting as a staged family system: values alignment, collaborative boundaries, Islamic principles, and practical tools: https://islamicparentingsolutions.com/

### UX/IA Patterns

- Information architecture is not only navigation. It defines content/functionality and the underlying organization, structure, and labels that connect them. Reference: Texas A&M summary of NN/g IA concepts, https://web.tamu.edu/aggie-ux-university/defining-and-organizing-your-sites-content/key-elements-of-user-experience/information-architecture/
- A sitemap is a planning artifact within IA, not the entire IA. UX Lift summarizes NN/g's distinction: https://www.uxlift.org/articles/information-architecture-vs-sitemaps---what-s-the-difference/
- Journey maps should include actor/persona, scenario, actions, thoughts, emotional state, pain points, and opportunities. Reference: Digital Public Services Wales journey map guidance, https://digitalpublicservices.gov.wales/guidance-and-standards/meet-user-needs/service-design-tools/journey-map
- Product pages matter because users often decide there whether to buy, and poor product-page content/layout causes abandonment. Baymard's PDP research argues product pages are central to ecommerce decision-making: https://baymard.com/research/product-page
- Product listing/category pages need clear scanning, filtering, and sorting support. Baymard frames product finding as critical because if users cannot find the product, they cannot buy it: https://baymard.com/research/ecommerce-product-lists
- Homepage/category navigation must quickly teach users what exists and how to find it. Baymard's homepage/category research centers product finding and site taxonomy: https://baymard.com/research/homepage-and-category-usability

## Competitive Gaps

Most adjacent products are one of these:

- Online Islamic school or class provider
- Islamic stories/content library
- Parenting advice subscription
- Single product shop
- Screen-free Islamic learning device/toy
- Behavior/salah/screen-time management system

brilliantroots can differentiate by being an ecosystem with a parent journey:

1. Parent clarity before child consumption.
2. Household rhythm before product purchase.
3. Product selection tied to a stage of tarbiyah.
4. Trust and methodology visible before conversion.
5. Gentle next steps instead of overwhelming content.

## Primary Audience

### Primary Persona: The Sincere but Scattered Parent

- Muslim mother or father with young children.
- Already values Islamic upbringing.
- Feels responsible but under-equipped.
- Has bought books/apps/cards before but struggles with consistency.
- Wants authenticity, practicality, and emotional calm.

### Secondary Persona: The Intentional Homeschool/Supplement Parent

- More proactive about curriculum.
- Wants structured pathways and repeatable resources.
- Needs age/stage clarity and implementation guidance.
- Likely to buy bundles, systems, and parent guides.

### Tertiary Persona: The Gift/Community Buyer

- Wants a trustworthy Islamic gift, device, book, or resource.
- Needs quick product clarity, social proof, and shipping/availability.
- Less interested in deep methodology until trust is established.

## Jobs To Be Done

- When I feel unsure how to raise my children Islamically day to day, help me find a clear starting point so I can act without overwhelm.
- When I see many Islamic products, help me understand which one fits my family stage so I do not buy noise.
- When I want to teach a child, help me turn beneficial knowledge into a routine, conversation, or practice.
- When I evaluate a resource, help me trust its methodology, sourcing, and suitability.
- When I am busy, help me make one faithful next step this week.

## Product Positioning

brilliantroots is not "another Islamic kids brand." It is a guided tarbiyah ecosystem for Muslim families, beginning with the parent and extending into tools, routines, books, digital resources, and community.

## Content Strategy Hypothesis

The site should avoid leading with product catalog logic. It should lead with the parent journey:

1. Name the parental struggle.
2. Offer a starting point.
3. Explain the framework.
4. Show the ecosystem.
5. Prove trust/methodology.
6. Invite a small action.
