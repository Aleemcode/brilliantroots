# brilliantroots Moodboard

Purpose: choose a new visual direction for brilliantroots before the next redesign pass.

The site should still follow `SiteCopy.md`: a product-led Islamic family brand with one public discovery layer, **Explore**, serving Muslim parents, prospective spouses, and families who want clarity before choosing products, tools, works, and guides.

The current direction is not yet landing emotionally. These moodboard lanes are intentionally different from one another so we can choose a stronger taste before building.

---

## Direction 1: The Muslim Home Library

### Feeling

Quiet, rooted, tactile, trustworthy, collected over time.

This should feel like a shelf in a thoughtful Muslim home: books, useful tools, notes, cards, and guides arranged with care. Less "website layout", more "family study table".

### Visual Language

- Warm ivory and paper beige base.
- Ink navy text.
- Muted olive or date-brown secondary notes.
- Gold used sparingly as a mark of care, not decoration.
- Fine paper grain.
- Thin, soft rules like notebook dividers.
- Cards that look like catalogue slips, book plates, or library index cards.

### UI Cues

- Explore cards feel like shelf entries.
- Product images sit on paper trays, not floating ecommerce cards.
- Pathway sections feel like annotated reading notes.
- Published works can feel like book spines, covers, or catalogue records.
- Testimonials should look like collected field notes, not marketing quotes.

### Palette

- Paper: `#F4E8D2`
- Warm surface: `#FFF8EB`
- Ink navy: `#001F51`
- Olive note: `#626B45`
- Date brown: `#6C4A2F`
- Soft gold: `#FFCB66`
- Hairline: `rgba(91, 64, 34, 0.16)`

### Typography Direction

- Large serif moments for warmth and reflection.
- Clean sans for navigation and product clarity.
- Mono only for metadata, catalogue numbers, and content type labels.

### Best For

brilliantroots as a serious, warm family brand with products and intellectual work.

### Risk

Can become too quiet or old-fashioned if images and hierarchy are not strong.

## Direction 2: Contemporary Islamic Editorial

### Feeling

Confident, modern, curated, intelligent, culturally grounded.

This direction should feel like a modern Muslim magazine or publishing house that also sells useful products. It is more editorial than domestic.

### Visual Language

- Strong asymmetric layouts.
- Big headlines with disciplined whitespace.
- Deep ink, cream, and one vivid accent.
- Editorial cards with issue numbers, sections, labels, and sharp content hierarchy.
- Product imagery handled like magazine still life.
- Occasional geometric hints inspired by Islamic proportion, but not obvious ornament.

### UI Cues

- Home hero feels like a magazine cover.
- Explore feels like a curated issue index.
- Pathway feels like an editorial series.
- Store/published works feel like a reading room.
- About feels like a manifesto, not a generic brand page.

### Palette

- Cream: `#F3E7D1`
- Ink: `#071426`
- Cobalt: `#1546A0`
- Brass: `#C79235`
- Clay: `#A45734`
- Soft divider: `rgba(7, 20, 38, 0.12)`

### Typography Direction

- Strong display sans or serif for big editorial headlines.
- Tighter section labels.
- Numbered modules and issue-like metadata.

### Best For

Making brilliantroots feel distinctive, serious, and memorable.

### Risk

Could feel too grown-up or publishing-led if product discovery is not made practical enough.

## Direction 3: Gentle Family Workshop

### Feeling

Practical, warm, helpful, hands-on, parent-friendly.

This direction is less editorial and more functional. It should feel like a calm workshop table where a parent can pick the right product, guide, or routine without feeling judged.

### Visual Language

- Softer paper colors.
- Friendly rounded panels, but not childish.
- Icons as functional tools.
- Product cards with clear “best for”, “format”, “status”, and “next step”.
- Less dramatic typography, more scannable blocks.
- Gentle color coding for product/tool/work/guide.

### UI Cues

- Explore page behaves like a guided catalogue.
- Product cards are practical decision aids.
- Pathway has staged learning cards.
- Contact page routes inquiries clearly.
- Testimonials are prompts until real proof exists.

### Palette

- Parchment: `#F7EEDB`
- Cream: `#FFF9EF`
- Trust navy: `#001F51`
- Growth green: `#2F8F65`
- Warm orange: `#D96E32`
- Soft blue: `#DCE8F7`
- Divider: `rgba(45, 35, 23, 0.13)`

### Typography Direction

- Clean sans-led interface.
- Serif only for mission statements or reflections.
- Metadata should be readable, not overly stylized.

### Best For

Making the site immediately understandable and commerce-ready.

### Risk

May feel less standout if not given one memorable visual motif.

## Direction 4: Sacred Minimal Catalogue

### Feeling

Restrained, premium, intentional, calm, almost museum-like.

This is the most minimal direction. The brand feels selective: fewer things shown, more meaning per object.

### Visual Language

- Very quiet warm background.
- Lots of whitespace.
- Fine borders and restrained dividers.
- Product images given room.
- Strong object-focused cards.
- No decorative patterns.
- Very clear copy.

### UI Cues

- Home hero is sparse and direct.
- Explore cards are large and object-first.
- Pathway is a calm reading experience.
- Published works feel like a selected archive.
- CTAs are few and deliberate.

### Palette

- Bone: `#F5EFE4`
- White paper: `#FFFDF8`
- Ink: `#101820`
- Muted stone: `#746C60`
- Single accent gold: `#D9A441`
- Divider: `rgba(16, 24, 32, 0.10)`

### Typography Direction

- Beautiful serif headline or refined sans headline.
- Smaller, precise body copy.
- Minimal metadata.

### Best For

Trust, restraint, and high perceived quality.

### Risk

May feel too quiet for a growing product ecosystem unless photography becomes excellent.

## Recommended Shortlist

I would not continue with the current warm-editorial attempt as-is. It is caught between directions.

The strongest options for brilliantroots are:

1. **Contemporary Islamic Editorial**
   - Best for standout and memorability.
   - Makes brilliantroots feel like more than a shop.
   - Strong fit for published works and intentionality.

2. **Gentle Family Workshop**
   - Best for clarity and product conversion.
   - Strong fit for parents who need direction.
   - Easier to scale into checkout and product detail pages.

3. **Muslim Home Library**
   - Best emotional fit for the brand name and mission.
   - Warmest and most rooted.
   - Needs strong photography or illustration to avoid feeling flat.

## My Recommendation

Use a hybrid:

**Contemporary Islamic Editorial structure + Muslim Home Library warmth + Gentle Family Workshop clarity.**

In practice:

- Home should feel editorial and memorable.
- Explore should feel practical and catalogue-like.
- Pathway should feel like guided counsel.
- Published Works should feel like a reading room.
- About should feel like a manifesto of intentionality.
- Contact should feel like a clear routing desk.

## Design Motif To Explore

Use the idea of **"shelves and notes"**:

- Shelf lines as soft horizontal dividers.
- Catalogue numbers on entries.
- Product images on warm paper trays.
- Pathway cards as parent notes.
- Published works as reading-room entries.
- Footer as a library index.

This gives brilliantroots a distinct metaphor without relying on generic Islamic ornament.

## What To Avoid Next

- Generic landing page sections.
- Cards that only look like SaaS cards with beige colors.
- Too many same-weight borders.
- Dark mode dividers above `rgba(255,255,255,0.06)`.
- Overusing uppercase metadata.
- Decorative patterns that do not help comprehension.
- Product grids without family-stage or “best for” context.

## Next Step

Before coding, choose one:

- **A. Home Library:** warmer, rooted, domestic.
- **B. Islamic Editorial:** bolder, more memorable, more publishing-led.
- **C. Family Workshop:** clearest, practical, commerce-ready.
- **D. Sacred Minimal:** premium, quiet, object-focused.
- **E. Hybrid:** editorial home, practical Explore, library warmth.
