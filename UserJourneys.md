# User Journeys

## Journey 1: First-Time Parent Who Feels Overwhelmed

Actor: Sincere but scattered parent.

Goal: Understand where to begin with tarbiyah at home.

Entry: Search, social link, community recommendation, or direct homepage visit.

### Home

- Sees hero: understands brilliantroots helps parents move from intention to rhythm.
- Reads problem/parent journey: feels recognized rather than judged.
- Clicks Start Where You Are.
- Emotion: relief, cautious trust.
- Risk: if copy feels too abstract, they leave.
- Opportunity: make first CTA plain and practical.

### Start Here

- Chooses "I need foundations."
- Receives recommended next steps: digest, parent guide, methodology page.
- Emotion: more oriented.
- Risk: too many options recreate overwhelm.
- Opportunity: offer one primary path and one optional path.

### Resources / Jumu'ah Digest

- Reads what the weekly reminder includes.
- Subscribes or previews a sample.
- Emotion: small commitment feels manageable.
- Risk: vague newsletter promise feels like more inbox noise.
- Opportunity: show a sample issue and "one household practice" format.

### Methodology

- Later returns to understand the deeper framework.
- Looks for Qur'an/Sunnah/Salaf grounding and review process.
- Emotion: trust grows.
- Risk: methodology becomes too long or defensive.
- Opportunity: use scannable principles and clear sourcing boundaries.

## Journey 2: Parent Looking For A Child-Ready Tool

Actor: Parent with a child ready for a resource.

Goal: Pick the right product without buying something random.

Entry: Product link, homepage, Instagram, WhatsApp.

### Home

- Skims hero and ecosystem.
- Opens product card.
- Emotion: curious.
- Risk: product cards do not explain family need.
- Opportunity: each product card should say "best for" and "helps with."

### Products

- Filters by need: memorization, names, parent formation, routines.
- Compares products.
- Emotion: evaluating.
- Risk: product catalog feels disconnected from tarbiyah method.
- Opportunity: organize by family job, not only product type.

### Product Detail

- Reads outcome, what is included, age/stage fit, how to use it.
- Checks authenticity/methodology notes and FAQs.
- Takes CTA: preorder, buy, join waitlist, or ask question.
- Emotion: confident or hesitant.
- Risk: missing practical usage details.
- Opportunity: include "how this fits into your week."

### Checkout / Follow-Up

- Completes purchase or preorder.
- Receives implementation guide.
- Emotion: hopeful.
- Risk: post-purchase drop-off.
- Opportunity: onboarding email with first-week routine.

## Journey 3: Homeschool / Structured Parent

Actor: Parent already seeking curriculum-level support.

Goal: Build a coherent Islamic learning rhythm.

Entry: Search for Islamic homeschool resources, parent forum, referral.

### Home

- Looks for structure and credibility.
- Clicks Method or Start Here.
- Emotion: analytical.
- Risk: landing page feels too brand-led.
- Opportunity: foreground framework and systems.

### Methodology

- Reviews parent-first approach, household rhythm, systems.
- Looks for age/stage guidance and scope.
- Emotion: "This may fit our home."
- Risk: too little detail on curriculum philosophy.
- Opportunity: add downloadable framework PDF.

### Systems

- Explores Adhkar, Salah, Adab, Aqidah systems.
- Compares what is available now versus coming soon.
- Emotion: planning.
- Risk: unclear sequencing.
- Opportunity: show recommended sequence and weekly cadence.

### Resources

- Downloads guides/routines.
- Subscribes to digest.
- Emotion: committed.
- Risk: resource library becomes unstructured.
- Opportunity: filters by age, stage, and family goal.

## Journey 4: Trust-Seeking Visitor

Actor: Parent cautious about authenticity.

Goal: Determine whether brilliantroots is safe to trust.

Entry: Product share, search, friend recommendation.

### Home

- Looks for methodology signals.
- Scrolls to Method & Trust or footer.
- Emotion: skeptical but interested.
- Risk: too much marketing language.
- Opportunity: make trust route visible in nav and product pages.

### About / Trust

- Reads mission, values, sourcing, review process.
- Looks for team/advisory details.
- Emotion: cautious confidence.
- Risk: vague "authentic" claims without process.
- Opportunity: explain what gets reviewed and by whom.

### Product Detail

- Checks whether the specific product follows the stated methodology.
- Reads FAQs.
- Emotion: decision-ready.
- Risk: trust content is generic and not product-specific.
- Opportunity: add a trust note per product.

## Journey 5: Community-Oriented Parent

Actor: Parent who wants companionship and reminders.

Goal: Join a low-noise Muslim family community.

Entry: Homepage, digest, WhatsApp invite.

### Home

- Reads journey and final CTA.
- Clicks Community or Subscribe.
- Emotion: seeking belonging.
- Risk: community feels vague or spammy.
- Opportunity: define what members receive and community etiquette.

### Community

- Reviews channels, benefits, expectations, boundaries.
- Joins WhatsApp/Telegram or email.
- Emotion: connected.
- Risk: privacy concerns or notification overload.
- Opportunity: offer email-first option and clear frequency.

### Ongoing

- Receives weekly reminder.
- Participates in prompts/challenges.
- Returns to resources/products when relevant.
- Emotion: supported.
- Risk: content cadence becomes inconsistent.
- Opportunity: maintain predictable weekly rhythm.

## Journey 6: Gift Buyer

Actor: Relative/friend buying for a Muslim family.

Goal: Find a trustworthy gift quickly.

Entry: Product page, gift guide, search.

### Products

- Looks for clear product categories and "best for" labels.
- Emotion: practical.
- Risk: too much methodology before product clarity.
- Opportunity: add gift guide and quick comparison.

### Product Detail

- Checks age fit, shipping, what's included, and gift suitability.
- CTA: buy or preorder.
- Emotion: confident if logistics are clear.
- Risk: missing delivery/returns info.
- Opportunity: add gifting, shipping, and returns modules.

## Page-by-Page Journey Requirements

### Home

- Must answer: What is this? Who is it for? Where do I start?
- Must route: Start Here, Products, Method, Digest.
- Emotional job: reduce overwhelm.

### Start Here

- Must answer: Which path matches my current need?
- Must route: Foundations, Rhythm, Tools, Method, Community.
- Emotional job: create momentum.

### Methodology

- Must answer: Why should I trust this approach?
- Must route: Digest, Products, About/Trust.
- Emotional job: create confidence.

### Products

- Must answer: Which tool fits my family?
- Must route: Product details, comparison, support.
- Emotional job: make choice easier.

### Product Detail

- Must answer: Is this for us, what does it include, how do we use it?
- Must route: Buy/preorder, related resource, FAQ/contact.
- Emotional job: remove uncertainty.

### Resources

- Must answer: What can I use this week?
- Must route: Digest, guides, routines, downloads.
- Emotional job: make practice feel possible.

### Community

- Must answer: What kind of community is this, and what will I receive?
- Must route: WhatsApp, Telegram, newsletter.
- Emotional job: make belonging feel safe.

### About / Trust

- Must answer: Who is behind this, what do they believe, how are resources reviewed?
- Must route: Contact, Methodology, Products.
- Emotional job: earn credibility.

### Contact

- Must answer: How do I ask a question or get help?
- Must route: email/form, FAQ, community.
- Emotional job: make support accessible.
