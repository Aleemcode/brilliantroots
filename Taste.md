# Taste

## Taste Direction

Refined, rooted, quiet, intentional.

brilliantroots should feel like a well-kept study, a family notebook, and a trusted teacher's counsel. It should not feel like a loud ecommerce shop, a generic SaaS landing page, or a children's cartoon app.

## What It Should Feel Like

- Calm enough for an overwhelmed parent.
- Structured enough for a planner.
- Warm enough for a family brand.
- Serious enough for sacred subject matter.
- Practical enough to act on today.

## What To Avoid

- Overuse of crescent/moon/mosque patterns as decoration.
- Loud gradients, glassmorphism, and trend-led SaaS visuals.
- Childish colors that make the brand feel like a toy store.
- Product grids that feel disconnected from the parent journey.
- Excessive religious ornament without functional meaning.
- Overly academic copy that makes parents feel inadequate.
- Dark-mode borders that look sharp, neon, or high-friction.

## Visual Taste

### Color

The base should remain warm and paper-like, but every selected theme should tint the environment:

- Navy: authoritative, scholarly, stable.
- Blue: open, digital, accessible.
- Green: growth, nurture, family rhythm.
- Orange: warmth, action, energy.

Use accent color in:

- CTAs.
- Small symbols.
- Soft panels.
- Border focus states.
- Badges.
- Icons.

Do not use all four accent colors simultaneously in ordinary content. The theme selection should create harmony.

### Surfaces

Surfaces should feel layered:

- Background: page atmosphere.
- Surface: readable content field.
- Surface strong: featured/tinted section.
- Card: individual object or choice.
- Dark band: decisive close or high-trust statement.

In dark mode, borders should be translucent and restrained. Shadows should mostly disappear.

### Typography

Headings should be confident and compact, but not aggressive. Body copy should be generous and readable.

Use the serif face sparingly for reflective quotes or intimate mission language.

### Imagery

Imagery should show real product context when possible:

- Actual products.
- Parent/child learning moments.
- Device/book/resource closeups.
- Calm household scenes.

Avoid generic stock photos, overly staged smiles, and dark atmospheric crops that obscure the product.

### Iconography

Lucide icons are acceptable for functional clarity. Use them as small signals, not decorative mascots.

Icon containers should use `br-primary-soft` and `br-primary` so they follow the selected theme.

## Copy Taste

Voice:

- Gentle
- Clear
- Parent-aware
- Confident
- Spiritually grounded

Copy should say:

- "Start where you are."
- "One faithful next step."
- "Tools that serve the path."
- "Parent clarity before product noise."

Copy should not say:

- "Transform your family overnight."
- "The ultimate hack."
- "Guaranteed results."
- "Never struggle again."

## Interaction Taste

Interactions should feel like opening a notebook or being gently guided:

- Soft reveals.
- Subtle card lift.
- Clear active states.
- No surprise animations.
- No movement that competes with reading.

## Decision Rule

When unsure, choose the version that makes a sincere parent feel:

1. Less overwhelmed.
2. More clear.
3. More trusting.
4. More able to take a small action.
